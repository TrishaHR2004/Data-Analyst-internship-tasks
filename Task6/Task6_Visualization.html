# Sort top 10 happiest countries
top10 = df.sort_values("Happiness Score", ascending=False).head(10)

plt.figure(figsize=(10,6))
plt.barh(top10["Country"], top10["Happiness Score"])
plt.xlabel("Happiness Score")
plt.ylabel("Country")
plt.title("Top 10 Happiest Countries (2015)")
plt.gca().invert_yaxis()
plt.grid(axis='x')

plt.show()

# The top-ranked countries show significantly higher happiness scores,
#indicating a strong relationship between socio-economic stability and overall well-being. 
#The score gap between the 1st and 10th ranked country highlights how small changes in GDP, health, and freedom can influence national happiness rankings.

# Sort by GDP for smooth trend line
gdp_trend = df.sort_values("Economy (GDP per Capita)")

plt.figure(figsize=(10,6))
plt.plot(
    gdp_trend["Economy (GDP per Capita)"],
    gdp_trend["Happiness Score"]
)

plt.xlabel("GDP per Capita")
plt.ylabel("Happiness Score")
plt.title("Trend: GDP per Capita vs Happiness Score (2015)")
plt.grid(True)

plt.show()

#The upward trend indicates a positive correlation between GDP per capita and happiness score. While economic growth improves happiness, 
#the curve flattens at higher GDP levels, suggesting diminishing returns beyond a certain income threshold.
